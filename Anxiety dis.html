What are Anxiety Disorders?

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders are a group of mental health conditions characterized by excessive fear, worry, or nervousness. Unlike normal stress or nervousness, symptoms are persistent, intense, and interfere with daily functioning. Common types include generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, and phobias.

Difference Between Normal Worry and Anxiety Disorder

Criterion Normal Worry Anxiety Disorder
Trigger Happens in specific stressful situations Occurs without clear reason or is exaggerated
Duration Temporary, resolves naturally Lasts ≥6 months, persistent
Severity & Impact Mild impact on daily life Strongly interferes with daily functioning
Thoughts Realistic concerns about actual problems Racing thoughts, catastrophizing, excessive fear
Response to Support Improves with reassurance and time Requires therapy or medication for improvement
Physical Symptoms Mild tension or restlessness Muscle tension, fatigue, sleep problems, panic attacks

Symptoms of Anxiety Disorders

Psychological Symptoms

  • Excessive and uncontrollable worry
  • Restlessness, feeling "on edge"
  • Irritability and mood swings
  • Difficulty concentrating or mind going blank
  • Racing thoughts and catastrophizing

Physical Symptoms

  • Muscle tension and aches
  • Fatigue and low energy
  • Sleep problems (difficulty falling or staying asleep)
  • Rapid heartbeat, shortness of breath
  • Sweating, trembling, dizziness

Behavioral Symptoms

  • Avoidance of anxiety-provoking situations
  • Procrastination due to anxiety
  • Seeking excessive reassurance
  • Nervous habits (nail biting, pacing)
  • Difficulty with decision making

Causes & Risk Factors

Biological Factors

  • Genetics and family history
  • Imbalance in neurotransmitters (serotonin, GABA)
  • Brain chemistry and structure differences
  • Medical conditions (thyroid problems, heart issues)

Psychological Factors

  • Negative thinking patterns
  • History of trauma or abuse
  • Personality traits (perfectionism, neuroticism)
  • Poor coping skills

Environmental Factors

  • Stressful life events
  • Family conflicts or relationship problems
  • Chronic illness or pain
  • Work or academic pressure
  • Financial stress

Treatment Options

Psychotherapy

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Exposure therapy, Relaxation training, Mindfulness-based therapies

Medications

SSRIs (sertraline, escitalopram), SNRIs, Benzodiazepines (short-term), Buspirone

Lifestyle Changes

Regular exercise, Healthy diet, Good sleep hygiene, Stress management, Limiting caffeine/alcohol

Complementary Approaches

Yoga, Meditation, Breathing exercises, Acupuncture, Support groups

Self-Help & When to Seek Help

Self-Help Strategies

  • Practice relaxation and mindfulness daily
  • Limit caffeine and alcohol intake
  • Maintain a consistent daily routine
  • Use journaling to manage anxious thoughts
  • Seek social support from friends and family
  • Gradual exposure to anxiety-provoking situations
  • Regular physical activity and exercise

When to Seek Help

  • If symptoms last ≥6 months
  • If anxiety interferes with work, school, or relationships
  • If panic attacks or physical symptoms are frequent
  • If suicidal thoughts occur
  • If self-help strategies aren't providing relief
  • If using substances to cope with anxiety
  • If experiencing significant distress or impairment
