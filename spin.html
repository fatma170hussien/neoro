SPIN Factor Structure

The SPIN measures three dimensions of social anxiety:

Fear Subscale (Items 1-6)
• Fear of authority figures
• Fear of criticism
• Fear of embarrassment
• Social anxiety symptoms
Avoidance Subscale (Items 7-12)
• Avoidance of social situations
• Avoidance of public speaking
• Avoidance of attention
• Avoidance behaviors
Physiological Arousal (Items 13-17)
• Blushing
• Sweating
• Heart palpitations
• Trembling
• Physiological symptoms

Psychometric Properties

Cutoff Scores: A score of 19 or higher has 89% sensitivity and 90% specificity for identifying Social Anxiety Disorder.

Reliability: Excellent internal consistency (α = .94) and test-retest reliability (r = .78-.89).

Validity: Strong correlations with other social anxiety measures and distinguishes between clinical and non-clinical populations.
